Tiffany D. Do (Diễm Đỗ)

Ph.D. Candidate at The University of Central Florida. Computer Science. XR/VR/HCI.


Photo by Vraj of EUCF

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Central Florida. My research interests are in HCI (Human-Computing Interactions), extended reality (VR/AR), and virtual avatars. I am currently working on research that is designed to investigate how technology, specifically mixed reality, can influence the behaviors and attitudes of people. I conduct research as a member of the eXtended Reality & Training Lab (XRT) under the supervision of Professor Ryan McMahan. Previously, I received my B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Dallas.

I am passionate about building systems and tech that can change how people interact with the world around them. As the proud daughter of a Vietnamese refugee family, I go by both my American name, Tiffany, and my native name, Diễm. I am very active in diversity in computing organizations, which you can find more about in service.

service & outreach


Girls Who Code @ UCF

As a woman of color in STEM, I am especially passionate about diversity in computing. I currently serve as a founding member and the External Vice-President of Girls Who Code @ UCF. Due to my love for coding, I also lead the technical interview prep group at GWC@UCF to help prepare our brilliant club members for industry jobs. I assign weekly Leetcode problems, help people work through them, and discuss optimal approaches to solving coding challenges. I also help prepare speakers and topics for technical workshops, and have even hosted some myself.

I have taught the following invited workshops for GWC:
Introduction to Git and GitHub
(coming soon!) Introduction to Unix

Previously, I have served as a mentor for the ACM-W (2020-2022) chapter at UCF. Additionally, I have served as an external reviewer for the following journals and conferences: Springer Virtual Reality (2021), ACM MM (2021, 2022), IEEE VR (2022), ACM CHI (2022, 2023), IEEE ISMAR (2022).
